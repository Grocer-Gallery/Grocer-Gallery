Description

The fruits are black to brown with sweet, fibrous pulp and each seed is enclosed within a woody endocarp. Young palmyra seedlings grow slowly, producing only a few leaves each year (establishment phase), but at an as yet undetermined time, they grow rapidly, producing a substantial stem.

Nutritional Facts

Calories: 36. Protein: 4 grams. Fat: less than 1 gram. Carbs: 4 grams. Fiber: 4 grams. Potassium: 38% of the Daily Value (DV) Phosphorus: 20% of the DV. Copper: 70% of the DV.

Benefits

Palm fruit has anti-inflammatory and anti-oxidant properties. The fruit pulp helps to cure skin inflammations. It is used to treat nausea and vomiting as well as worm infestation. It is used as an expectorant and also as a liver tonic.
